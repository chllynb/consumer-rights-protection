# Mainland China Consumer Rights Legal Basis

Use this file when the user needs legal framing, regulator-facing language, or compensation arguments.

Important:

- This is a working reference for drafting and triage, not a formal legal opinion.
- Before sending a final complaint in a high-value or high-risk case, the latest official law text should control.
- Cite only the narrowest rules that help the user's case.

## Common Legal Theories

### False or misleading advertising

Use when the product page, booking page, livestream, menu, poster, or sales pitch materially differs from reality.

Key points:

- Operators should provide true, complete, and accurate information about goods or services.
- If the merchant highlights an important condition but hides the limiting condition, that supports a misleading-advertising argument.
- On e-commerce platforms, inaccurate listing information can also support a platform-rule complaint.

Primary rules commonly cited:

- Consumer Rights Protection Law, Article 20
- E-commerce Law, Article 17
- Measures for Penalties for Infringing Consumer Rights and Interests, Article 6

### Unfair standard terms

Use when the merchant relies on pre-set clauses such as:

- "Special-price items are non-refundable"
- "No cancellation after booking"
- "Free cancellation before 18:00, non-refundable after 18:00"
- "Final interpretation belongs to the store"
- "No compensation under any circumstance"

Key points:

- A merchant cannot use standard terms to unfairly exclude or limit consumer rights.
- If the term unreasonably shifts risk to the consumer or exempts the merchant from its own fault, the term may be ineffective.

Primary rules commonly cited:

- Consumer Rights Protection Law, Article 26
- Civil Code, Article 497

### Hotel cancellation cutoff clauses

Use when a hotel, OTA, or homestay platform relies on a clause such as:

- "Free cancellation before 18:00 on check-in day"
- "Non-refundable after 18:00"
- "Holiday orders cannot be cancelled once confirmed"

Key points:

- A fixed cutoff time is usually a business rule, not a statutory line created by law.
- The clause is stronger when the page clearly disclosed it before payment and the booking information itself was accurate.
- The clause is weaker when the platform or hotel omitted a material condition, used misleading room information, changed availability, or otherwise caused the booking purpose to fail.
- If the merchant's own listing problem triggered the dispute, frame the case around misleading information and unfair reliance on a standard term.

Practical argument:

- The consumer is not merely changing their mind after the cutoff.
- The transaction could not be performed as a normal consumer would reasonably understand from the listing.
- In that situation, the merchant or platform should not mechanically use the 18:00 cutoff to avoid refund responsibility.

### Product or service defects

Use when the item is damaged, counterfeit, missing, materially inconsistent, or the service fails basic quality requirements.

Key points:

- The consumer may seek return, replacement, repair, or other contract remedies depending on the facts.
- Necessary costs related to compliant return or repair may also be arguable.

Primary rules commonly cited:

- Consumer Rights Protection Law, Article 24

### Fraud and punitive damages

Use carefully. Do not label every dispute as fraud.

Good indicators:

- The merchant knowingly made false statements to induce purchase.
- The merchant knowingly sold fake, defective, or impossible-to-perform goods or services.
- The merchant took payment while never intending to provide the promised service.

Primary rules commonly cited:

- Consumer Rights Protection Law, Article 55

Practical note:

- If fraud is not strongly supported, frame the case first as misleading advertising, breach, or unfair terms.

### Food safety

Use when there is spoilage, contamination, foreign matter, expired food, or illness linked to the food.

Key points:

- Preserve the food, package, receipt, and medical records.
- Food-safety claims often depend on whether the product fails food-safety standards and whether actual harm occurred.

Primary rules commonly cited:

- Food Safety Law, Article 148

### Failure to perform or closure after taking prepaid funds

Use when a gym, beauty salon, education provider, or other merchant closes, relocates, materially reduces services, or refuses refund after taking prepaid money.

Key points:

- The dispute may involve breach, inability to continue performance, and unfair terms.
- If the merchant appears to have collected money while unable or unwilling to perform, the complaint should stress ongoing harm and the risk to multiple consumers.

Common support points:

- Civil Code rules on contract performance and termination
- Consumer Rights Protection Law rules on unfair terms and truthful information

## Drafting Rules

### Good regulator-facing language

- "The advertised conditions materially differed from the actual conditions."
- "The merchant relied on a pre-formulated clause to deny the consumer's main rights."
- "The consumer preserved screenshots, order records, payment records, and communication records."
- "The consumer requests refund and handling of the suspected unlawful conduct."

### Avoid over-claiming

Avoid these unless well supported:

- "This is definitely criminal fraud"
- "The platform must pay me ten times"
- "Any clause I dislike is automatically void"

## Ready-To-Use Issue Labels

Use one or two concise labels in drafts:

- `虚假宣传 / 引人误解宣传`
- `格式条款不公平`
- `商品或服务与约定不符`
- `拒绝履行退款或退换义务`
- `食品安全风险`
- `预付费后无法继续履约`
- `机械适用 18:00 退改截止规则`

## Complaint Sentence Templates

### For misleading advertising

`商家在交易前以页面或宣传内容明确展示[承诺内容]，交易后实际情况却为[实际情况]，足以影响普通消费者判断，涉嫌构成虚假或引人误解的宣传。`

### For unfair terms

`商家援引“[条款内容]”拒绝退款、取消或赔付，但该条款系单方预设，实质上减轻自身责任、限制消费者主要权利，存在格式条款不公平问题。`

### For 18:00 hotel cutoff disputes

`本案并非消费者在知悉真实条件后单纯逾期反悔，而是因页面对[房型/入住人数/限制条件]展示不准确或披露不充分，导致订单目的无法实现。商家或平台机械适用“18:00 后不可退”规则，存在不当限制消费者权利的问题。`

### For fraud claim with caution

`如监管核查认为商家在明知无法按约提供商品或服务的情况下仍收取消费款，请依法审查是否涉及欺诈及相应赔偿责任。`

## Commonly Used Article Pointers

These article numbers are frequently relevant in consumer cases:

- Consumer Rights Protection Law: Articles 20, 24, 26, 55
- Civil Code: Article 497; contract-termination rules may also matter depending on facts
- E-commerce Law: Article 17
- Food Safety Law: Article 148
- Measures for Penalties for Infringing Consumer Rights and Interests: Articles 5 and 6
